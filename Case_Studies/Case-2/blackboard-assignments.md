## Question 3: AI-Assisted Financial & Risk Analysis (10 points)

This question requires you to demonstrate effective AI collaboration for business analysis.

**Instructions**: 

Use an AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or another) to help you understand the financial and risk dimensions of this decision. Show us your prompting skills and critical thinking.

**Submit**:

1. **Three prompts you used** to analyze this case with AI
   - At least one prompt about financial analysis (costs, ROI, payback period)
   - At least one prompt about risk assessment (what could go wrong)
   - At least one prompt that challenges assumptions or asks "what if" questions

2. **For each prompt, include**:
   - The full prompt text (exactly what you asked)
   - WHY you asked this question (what you hoped to learn) - 2-3 sentences
   - WHAT you learned from AI's response - 2-3 sentences
   - Critical evaluation: Did the AI miss anything important? What would you probe deeper on? - 2-3 sentences

3. **Your synthesis** (150-200 words):
   - Based on AI's help, which option makes the most financial sense over 3 years?
   - What risks did AI help you identify that weren't obvious from reading the case?
   - What did AI get wrong or what assumptions should you question?

**What Makes a Strong Prompt for This Assignment**:

‚úÖ **DO**:
- Provide context: "I'm evaluating 5 options for a $180M retailer..."
- Ask for analysis, not decisions: "Help me understand..." not "What should I do?"
- Request AI to challenge assumptions: "What factors might make Option 2 more expensive than projected?"
- Show iterative thinking: Follow-up questions that build on AI's previous answers
- Explain your purpose: "I'm asking this because I need to understand..."

‚ùå **DON'T**:
- Ask AI to make your decision: "Which option should we choose?"
- Be vague: "Tell me about AI in marketing"
- Just summarize the case: AI should help you understand, not repeat what's already written
- Accept AI's answer without critical evaluation

**Example of Strong Prompt**:
> "I'm evaluating whether a mid-size retailer should build internal AI capability ($1.2M Year 1, 18-24 months) vs. buy vendor solution ($850K Year 1, 3 months deployment). The vendor solution is faster and cheaper Year 1, but building internal gives us full control and owns IP. Help me think through: What hidden costs might make the 'build' option even more expensive than projected? Consider talent retention, infrastructure scaling, and opportunity costs. Then challenge your own analysis‚Äîwhat might make internal build cheaper than it appears?"

**Example of Weak Prompt**:
> "Compare the costs of the five options."

**Grading Note**:
- This question assesses **Prompt Sophistication** (70%) and **AI Collaboration** (30%)
- See Master Rubrics Sections 1 and 3
- We grade the quality of your PROMPTS, not the quality of AI's answers
- Strong prompts are specific, strategic, and show critical thinking

**üìã Rubric Reminder**: Review Prompt Quality Rubric (Section 1) before writing your prompts!

**Writing Guide**: 3 prompts + explanations + synthesis = approximately 600-800 words total

## Academic Integrity Note

This is an individual assignment. You may (and should!) use AI tools to help you think through the issues‚Äîthat's explicitly what we're asking in Question 3. However:

**Acceptable AI Use**:
- ‚úÖ Asking AI to help you understand financial concepts
- ‚úÖ Using AI to generate alternatives you might not have considered
- ‚úÖ Asking AI to challenge your thinking or identify blind spots
- ‚úÖ Using AI to explain case elements you don't understand

**Unacceptable AI Use**:
- ‚ùå Having AI write your answers and copying them verbatim
- ‚ùå Asking AI "What should I answer for Question 1?" and using its response
- ‚ùå Not synthesizing AI insights into your own words and analysis
- ‚ùå Unclear what's AI's thinking vs. your own thinking

**The test**: If someone asked you to explain your answer without looking at notes, could you? If you couldn't explain it because AI wrote it, that's a problem.

**Use AI as a thinking partner, not a replacement for your own thinking.**

When in doubt: **Attribute AI's help explicitly** ("I asked Claude about X and learned...") and **synthesize in your own words**.

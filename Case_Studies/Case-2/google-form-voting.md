## Section 3: Post-Deliberation Position

**Help Text**: "After hearing arguments from fellow board members and reviewing the breaking news about CustomerIQ, what is your final vote?"

**Question 5: Final Board Vote (Post-Deliberation)**
- Type: Multiple choice
- Required: Yes
- Options:
  - Option 1: Buy Full Vendor Solution (CustomerIQ™ Pro)

  - Option 3: Hybrid Approach (Vendor + Internal Team)
  - Option 4: Start Small - Pilot with Vendor
  - Option 5: Enhanced Manual Personalization (No AI Yet)

**Question 6: Did You Change Your Vote?**
- Type: Multiple choice
- Required: Yes
- Options:

- Type: Paragraph

## Confirmation Message (After Submission)

**Title**: "Vote Recorded - Thank You!"

**Message**:
"Your board vote has been recorded. You should receive a copy of your responses at your email address.

**What Happens Next**:
1. The instructor will tally votes and determine the board's decision
2. You'll learn the consequences of the board's choice at the start of the next session
3. There will be a brief reflection on how it worked out

**Remember**: We grade on the quality of your reasoning, not on whether the board's decision worked out well. Good decisions can have bad outcomes due to uncertainty—that's the nature of business decision-making.

See you at the next board meeting!"

## Data Analysis Notes for Instructor

**Key Metrics to Track**:
1. Pre vs. Post deliberation vote shifts (Question 3 vs. Question 5)
2. Impact of breaking news (Question 8)
3. Vote change rate (Question 6)
4. Reasons for changing votes (Question 7)
5. Distribution of primary decision factors (Question 10)
6. Application of Case 1 learnings (Question 14)
7. AI tool usage during deliberation (Questions 17-19)
8. Deliberation quality ratings (Question 22)

**Vote Distribution**:
- Track which option wins
- Identify any consensus emerging
- Note any strong minority positions
- Compare to Case 1 voting patterns

**Learning Progression**:
- Are students using more sophisticated reasoning than Case 1?
- Is AI tool usage during deliberation increasing?
- Are students engaging more with each other's arguments?

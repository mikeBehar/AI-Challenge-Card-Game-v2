# AI Challenge Card Game - Student Guide

## What is the AI Challenge Card Game?

An interactive, team-based learning activity where you work together to solve AI-related challenges. You'll practice critical thinking, prompt engineering, argumentation, and risk assessment through real-world scenarios.

- Include relevant context

**Challenge:** Predict what could go wrong with an AI system

**Tips:**

## How the Game Works

### 1. Form Teams (3-5 students)
- Assigned by instructor
- Work together throughout game
- Contribute to discussion
- One response per team

### 2. Receive Challenge Card
- Read scenario carefully
- Discuss as team
- Plan response together
- Designate someone to write

### 3. Work Time (varies by card type)
- Use full time available
- Quality > Speed
- Show your thinking
- Check your work

### 4. Submit Response
- Written in shared document OR
- Verbal presentation (debates)
- Submit before time ends
- Can't revise after submission

### 5. Grading & Feedback
- Instructor grades immediately
- Announces score (0-10 points)
- Gives brief justification
- Reveals answer (if applicable)

### 6. Learning Moment
- See what you missed
- Understand better approaches
- Connect to course concepts
- Improve for next round

## Scoring

### Point Scale (All Card Types)
- **10 points maximum** per card
- **9-10 (Excellent):** Sophisticated, comprehensive, demonstrates mastery
- **7-8 (Good):** Solid response with minor gaps
- **5-6 (Fair):** Basic response with significant gaps
- **0-4 (Poor):** Inadequate or misunderstands task

### What Earns High Scores?

**Prompt Cards:**
- Clear, specific, actionable
- Includes context and constraints
- Addresses potential limitations
- Specifies output format

**Debate Cards:**
- 3+ strong supporting points
- Addresses counterarguments
- Uses evidence and examples
- Persuasive presentation

**Prediction Cards:**
- 2-3 sophisticated risks identified
- Clear reasoning about mechanisms
- Shows understanding of systemic issues
- Depth of analysis

**Risk Assessment Cards:**
- 12+ risks across all 6 categories
- Appropriate severity ratings
- Comprehensive coverage
- Framework mastery

## Time Management

### Use Your Time Wisely:
1. **First 1/3:** Understand challenge, discuss options
2. **Middle 1/3:** Draft response, develop ideas
3. **Last 1/3:** Refine, improve, check quality

### Don't:
- Submit immediately (use full time)
- Wait until last minute to start
- Panic when time is running out
- Sacrifice quality for speed

---

## Important Rules

### During Work Time:
- Work with your team only
- No phones (unless for research if allowed)
- Ask instructor for clarifications
- Use framework reference (for Risk Assessments)

### During Grading:
- Listen to other teams' scores
- Learn from instructor feedback
- Note what worked well
- Apply lessons to next round

### General:
- Be respectful of all ideas
- Focus on learning, not just winning
- Take feedback constructively
- Have fun while learning!

## Strategy for Success

### Before Each Round:
1. Read scenario twice
2. Identify key issues
3. Discuss as team before writing
4. Assign roles (reader, writer, timekeeper)

### During Response:
1. Answer the actual question asked
2. Show your reasoning
3. Be specific, not generic
4. Use course concepts appropriately

### After Feedback:
1. Note what instructor emphasized
2. Understand what you missed
3. Apply to next round
4. Ask questions if unclear
